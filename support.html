This usually happens when two or more settings conflict with each other in a way that makes a mathematically fair lineup impossible. The most common combination is:

  • GK rotation is on — so one goalkeeper plays exactly one half in goal
  • Playing in halves — meaning that GK uses up half their total game time in goal
  • GK time counted as bonus time — meaning their GK half doesn't count toward their fair share of outfield minutes

The problem: if GK time is bonus, that player still needs a full fair share of outfield time on top of their half in goal — which would require them to play more than 100% of the game. That's not possible, so allPlay can't build a balanced lineup.

How to fix it: Try one of these adjustments in your settings —
  • Turn off GK time as bonus, so the goalkeeper's time in goal counts toward their fair share
  • Switch from halves to quarters, giving more flexibility to distribute time
  • Turn off GK rotation if you have a dedicated goalkeeper who always plays in goal

In general, if allPlay can't generate a lineup, try relaxing one setting at a time until it succeeds. The app will usually indicate which setting combination is causing the conflict.

Your purchase is tied to your Apple ID, not your device. If you've reinstalled the app, switched to a new device, or logged in with the same Apple ID on another device, you can restore your purchase at no charge.

To restore: Look for a Restore Purchase option in the app's settings or on the unlock screen. Tap it and sign in with the same Apple ID you used when you originally purchased. Apple will confirm the purchase and unlock full functionality automatically.

If restoring doesn't work, make sure you're signed into the correct Apple ID — the one you used when you originally bought the unlock.

From the Roster screen, tap the + button to add a new player. Enter their name and optionally set position preferences — these tell allPlay which positions that player prefers or should be prioritised for during lineup generation.

Position preferences are per-game, so you can adjust them before generating each new lineup without affecting your base roster.

Tip: If a player has a strong preference for goalkeeper (or you want to avoid putting them there), setting that preference explicitly will give you more predictable results.

allPlay supports formats from 5v5 through to 11v11, covering the full range of youth recreational soccer. Within each format you can choose from a variety of standard formations.

Once you've generated a lineup, tap Text PDF for a print-ready game sheet showing each period's lineup and substitution plan, or Field PDF for a visual field diagram. Both are generated on your device and can be shared via AirDrop, email, or printed directly.

Tip: The field PDF is great for sharing with assistant coaches on the sideline who prefer a visual overview.

Tap Save Lineup on the lineup screen. Saved lineups appear under the Saved tab in the navigation bar, where you can review or reload any previous game's lineup.

All your data — rosters, lineups, and settings — is stored locally on your device only. It is never uploaded to any server.

If you back up your device to iCloud or your Mac, your allPlay data will be included in that backup and will transfer to a new device through a normal restore. Deleting the app will remove all local data, so export any lineups you want to keep before uninstalling.
